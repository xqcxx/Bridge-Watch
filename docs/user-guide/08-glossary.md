# Glossary of Terms

## A

**Aggregation**
The process of combining data from multiple sources to provide a unified view. Bridge Watch aggregates liquidity from multiple DEXs and prices from multiple sources.

**Alert**
A notification triggered when specific conditions are met, such as price deviations or bridge supply mismatches.

**AMM (Automated Market Maker)**
A type of decentralized exchange protocol that uses liquidity pools instead of order books.

**Asset**
A digital token on the Stellar network, either native (XLM) or issued by a specific issuer.

## B

**Bridge**
A system that enables the transfer of assets between different blockchain networks (e.g., Ethereum to Stellar).

**Bridge Operator**
An entity responsible for managing and maintaining a cross-chain bridge.

**Bridged Asset**
An asset that has been transferred from another blockchain to Stellar via a bridge.

## C

**Circuit Breaker**
An automated safety mechanism that pauses operations when anomalies are detected.

**Cooldown Period**
The minimum time that must pass before an alert can trigger again.

## D

**Depeg**
When a stablecoin's price deviates significantly from its intended peg (usually $1.00).

**DEX (Decentralized Exchange)**
A peer-to-peer marketplace where users can trade cryptocurrencies without intermediaries.

## H

**Health Score**
A composite metric (0-100) that evaluates the overall health of an asset based on liquidity, price stability, bridge uptime, reserves, and volume.

**Horizon**
Stellar's REST API for interacting with the Stellar network.

## L

**Liquidity**
The availability of an asset for trading. Higher liquidity means easier buying/selling with less price impact.

**Liquidity Depth**
The volume of buy and sell orders at various price levels.

**Liquidity Pool**
A collection of funds locked in a smart contract, used to facilitate trading on AMMs.

## M

**Merkle Proof**
A cryptographic proof used to verify that specific data is part of a larger dataset without revealing the entire dataset.

**Mismatch**
A discrepancy between expected and actual values, such as supply on Stellar vs. source chain.

## O

**OHLC**
Open, High, Low, Close - standard price data points for a given time period.

## P

**Peg**
The target price that a stablecoin aims to maintain (e.g., $1.00 for USD stablecoins).

**Price Deviation**
The difference between prices from different sources or from an expected value.

**Price Oracle**
A service that provides price data from external sources to blockchain applications.

## R

**Reserve**
Assets held by a bridge operator to back bridged tokens on another chain.

**Reserve Backing**
The ratio of reserves held to tokens issued, ideally 1:1 or higher.

**RWA (Real World Asset)**
Traditional financial assets (like money market funds) tokenized on blockchain.

## S

**SDEX (Stellar Decentralized Exchange)**
Stellar's built-in decentralized exchange.

**Slippage**
The difference between expected and actual trade execution price.

**Soroban**
Stellar's smart contract platform.

**Spread**
The difference between the highest bid price and the lowest ask price.

**Stablecoin**
A cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value relative to a reference asset (usually USD).

**Supply**
The total amount of an asset in circulation.

## T

**Threshold**
A predefined value that triggers an action when crossed (e.g., alert threshold).

**TVL (Total Value Locked)**
The total value of assets deposited in a protocol or liquidity pool.

## V

**VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)**
An average price weighted by trading volume, giving more weight to prices with higher volume.

**Verification**
The process of confirming that bridge reserves match issued tokens.

## W

**Webhook**
An HTTP callback that sends real-time data to a specified URL when an event occurs.
